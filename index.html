Canadian Population Aged 65+ Projections

Current 65+ Population

~8.1 Million

Approximately 8.1 million Canadians are currently aged 65 and over, representing a large and growing addressable market for audiology services. ~65% of individuals aged 70–79 experience hearing loss.

2030 Projection

~9.3 Million

The 65+ population is expected to reach ~9.3M by 2030, representing a ~15% increase from current levels and significantly expanding the addressable market for hearing healthcare.

2040 Projection

~10.5 Million

By 2040, the 65+ population is projected to reach ~10.5M, a ~30% increase from today. Combined with significant underdiagnosis, this supports strong long-term demand for audiology services.

Multi-Brand Audiology Platform

An established multi-brand platform with a long operating history that spans multiple decades. The multi-brand approach provides diversification and broad market coverage across Western Canada's audiology sector.

Multi-Clinic Footprint

Multi-clinic footprint across strategic markets in Western Canada. The geographic spread provides both resilience and a strong base for further expansion into adjacent markets.

Recurring Revenue Model

Recurring revenue is driven by predictable replacement cycles (hearing aids typically replaced every 3–5 years) and ongoing service needs including fitting adjustments, maintenance, and follow-up appointments.

Loyal Customer Base

Long-standing customer relationships built over decades of operation. Customer loyalty in audiology is high due to the personalized nature of hearing care and the trust-based relationship between audiologists and patients.

Experienced Operations Team

Experienced team in place to support day-to-day operations, providing continuity and operational stability for a new owner. The team's deep industry knowledge and established processes reduce transition risk.

Established Market Presence

Strong local market presence in Western Canada with multi-decade operating history. Six consecutive record revenue years demonstrate consistent demand and market position strength.

High Margins

76% average gross margin since FY2022 and ~28% EBITDA margin ($2.0M) demonstrate strong unit economics. The audiology sector benefits from high-value products, professional service delivery, and limited price competition from online channels.

Recurring Revenue

Predictable replacement cycles (every 3–5 years) and ongoing service needs create natural recurring revenue streams. Evidence: consistent revenue growth from FY2020–FY2025 across 6 record years.

Fragmented Market — Roll-Up Opportunity

The Canadian audiology market is highly fragmented with numerous independent operators, presenting a clear acquisition and roll-up opportunity. The platform's existing multi-brand infrastructure is well-positioned to integrate additional clinics efficiently.

Government-Supported Demand

Provincial government programs across Canada subsidize hearing aid purchases, increasing accessibility and reducing price sensitivity for end consumers. This government support creates a more stable demand environment compared to purely consumer-funded healthcare segments.

Capacity Expansion

Clinics are currently operating at ~80% capacity. Near-term upside can be realized through extended operating hours, hiring additional practitioners, and optimizing appointment scheduling — all without significant capital expenditure.

Clinic Acquisitions (Roll-Up)

The Canadian audiology market is highly fragmented with numerous independent operators. The established multi-clinic platform is well-positioned to execute a roll-up strategy, acquiring independent clinics and integrating them onto the existing operational platform for immediate synergies.

Geographic Expansion

The existing Western Canadian footprint provides a strong base for expansion into adjacent geographic markets, leveraging the established operational playbook and brand recognition.

Brand & Operational Optimization

Opportunity to streamline branding across the multi-brand platform and enhance operational efficiencies at each location through standardized processes, shared services, and technology investments.

Brand Consolidation

Unifying the multi-brand portfolio under a consolidated brand identity enhances scalability, market recognition, and marketing efficiency while reducing operational complexity.
